# What is the Difference Between Skin Tone and Undertone?

It is the most common fashion frustration: you buy a beautiful shirt in a color that constantly gets compliments on your friend, but when you put it on, your skin looks tired, washed out, or even slightly green. Why does this happen? The answer lies in the fundamental difference between **skin tone** and **undertone**.

While they might sound like the same thing, confusing these two concepts is the primary reason people buy clothes they end up never wearing. 

## What is Skin Tone? (The Surface Color)

Your **skin tone** (or complexion) is the surface color of your skin. It is determined exclusively by the amount of melanin present in the uppermost layers of your epidermis. 

In India, skin tone is typically broadly categorized into:
- Fair 
- Medium / Wheatish
- Dusky / Bronze
- Deep / Dark

**The defining characteristic of your skin tone is that it can change.** If you spend a week on a sunny beach in Goa, your skin tone will darken (tan). In the winter, it may become lighter. If you have acne or rosacea, your surface tone may appear redder. 

*Rule of thumb:* Skin tone tells you how light or dark a foundation you need, but it **does not** tell you what colors you should wear.

## What is an Undertone? (The Sub-Surface Hue)

Your **undertone** is the subtle, muted hue underneath the surface of your skin. It is determined by the combination of melanin, carotene (a yellowish pigment), and hemoglobin (red blood vessels) beneath the epidermis. 

Unlike your surface skin tone, **your undertone never changes.** It doesn't matter if you have a deep winter tan or if you haven't seen the sun in months; your undertone is genetically locked in.

Undertones are classified into three distinct categories:
1. **Warm:** Golden, yellow, or peachy hues beneath the skin.
2. **Cool:** Pink, red, or bluish hues beneath the skin.
3. **Neutral:** A perfect balance—neither overly golden nor overly pink. Some neutral tones also lean "Olive," presenting a slight greenish/yellowish quality.

## Why Undertones Are the Secret to Great Fashion

Have you ever wondered why silver jewelry makes you look radiant but gold makes you look pale? Or why bright orange makes your skin pop, but icy blue makes you look exhausted? This is color theory interacting with your *undertone*, not your surface tone.

- **Warm undertones** harmonize beautifully with earth tones, warm reds, mustard yellows, and golds.
- **Cool undertones** come alive in jewel tones like sapphire blue, emerald green, deep purples, and silvers.
- **Neutral undertones** have the flexibility to wear colors from both spectrums, often looking best in muted or soft versions of any color.

Two people can have the exact same "wheatish" surface skin tone, but if one has a warm undertone and the other has a cool undertone, they will look completely different when wearing the exact same pink dress.
