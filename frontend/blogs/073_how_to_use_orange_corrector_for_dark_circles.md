# How to Use Orange Corrector for Dark Circles?

If you possess a medium to deep Indian skin tone, you have likely experienced the agony of dark under-eye circles. 

You do what everyone else does: you buy an expensive, bright highlighting concealer and apply it directly under your eyes to cover the darkness. The result? The dark circles completely turn completely gray, your eyes look ashy and bruised, and you look wildly exhausted. 

You cannot cover profound Indian hyperpigmentation with a light beige concealer. You must aggressively neutralize the wavelength. Your ultimate, indispensable weapon is the **Orange Color Corrector**. Here is the advanced masterclass on how to deploy it flawlessly.

## The Physics of Hyperpigmentation

Why orange? In deeply melanated skin, the dark circles under the eyes and the shadows surrounding the mouth are biologically composed of heavily concentrated melanin sitting beneath extremely thin dermal tissue. 

When light bounces through this thin skin, these areas project a distinct **Blue or dark Purple** optical wavelength. 

If you apply a white-based, pale concealer over a deep blue shadow, the white literally dilutes the blue. White + Blue = **Gray**. Your under-eyes look ashy and dead. 

Look at the Color Wheel. The mathematical, exact opposite of Blue is Orange. Therefore, to make Blue visually disappear, you must directly collide it with Orange pigment.

## The Execution Protocol

Executing the orange corrector hack requires surgical precision. If you use too much, your eyes look orange. If you use too little, the gray returns. 

### Step 1: Select the Proper Depth
Not all orange correctors are equal. 
- If you have **Medium/Wheatish** skin, use a soft **Peach** or Apricot corrector.
- If you have **Deep/Dusky** skin, use a heavy, highly saturated **Red-Orange** or Brick corrector. 

### Step 2: Localized Application
Do not smear the corrector everywhere. Look closely in the mirror and identify the exact boundary of the blue shadow. Dab a minuscule amount of the pure orange corrector *only* onto the darkest blue zones (usually the inner corner and strict undereye bag). Do not bring it down your cheek. 

### Step 3: The Crucial Set
Tap the orange corrector into the skin with a damp sponge until it is seamless. **Do immediately apply concealer.** If you apply wet concealer over wet orange corrector, the colors will mix, and your whole base will turn peach. 
*The Hack:* Set the tiny layer of orange corrector with a microscopic dust of translucent powder. This locks the orange pigment in place. 

### Step 4: The Skin-Tone Coverage
Now, apply your normal skin-matching foundation or a slightly brighter concealer directly over the locked orange corrector. The dark circles will have miraculously vanished into a seamless, flawless glow.
