# What is the 60-30-10 Rule of Fashion?

If you have ever walked into a beautifully designed hotel lobby and felt instantly calm, luxurious, and impressed—but couldn't explain *why*—you have experienced the effects of the **60-30-10 Rule**. 

Originally an interior design principle, the 60-30-10 rule was rapidly adopted by elite fashion stylists as the golden ratio for perfectly balanced outfits. It provides a mathematical formula that guarantees color harmony in any outfit, removing all guesswork. Here is exactly how it works.

## The Formula

The 60-30-10 rule states that any aesthetically perfect visual composition must be divided into three color proportions:

### 60% — The Dominant Color (The Foundation)
This is the color that covers the largest surface area of your outfit. It is your trousers, suit, dress, or primary garment. Because it covers the majority of your body, this color must be a highly flattering, biologically harmonious neutral or deep tone.
*Example:* A deep Navy Blue suit covering your entire torso and legs.

### 30% — The Secondary Color (The Support)
This color provides visual contrast and interest without overwhelming the Dominant color. It typically covers your shirt, blouse, or a major accessory like a jacket.
*Example:* A crisp Ivory white shirt beneath the Navy suit, creating a beautiful contrast while supporting the foundation.

### 10% — The Accent Color (The Pop)
This is a small, highly saturated burst of a bold, eye-catching color. Think of a tie, a pocket square, a statement necklace, a belt, or a pair of socks. This tiny injection of brightness completes the outfit, preventing it from looking flat or boring.
*Example:* A rich Burgundy silk tie or a deep Emerald pocket square pinned on the Navy suit.

## Why the Ratio Works

The 60-30-10 ratio mirrors how the human eye naturally processes visual information. If you wear three colors in exactly equal proportions (33/33/33), the eye cannot determine a visual hierarchy. It bounces chaotically between all three, creating fatigue and "noise."

By creating an overwhelming majority of one color (60%), you provide the eye with a calm resting place. The 30% creates enough contrast to maintain interest. The 10% accent provides chemical excitement, like the final seasoning on a meal.

## Applying the Rule to Indian Skin

The critical variable is ensuring all three colors match your biological undertone. The 60% neutral *must* be one of your Season's anchor neutrals (Camel for Autumns, Charcoal for Winters). The 10% accent *must* be your genetic Power Color (Mustard for Warm, Sapphire for Cool). If you mix temperatures across the three layers, the mathematical harmony of the ratio is destroyed.
