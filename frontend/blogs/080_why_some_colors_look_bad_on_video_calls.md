# Why Do Some Colors Look Bad on Video Calls?

We have all experienced the shock of looking at our webcam preview before a Zoom or Teams meeting. You looked perfectly healthy in the mirror five minutes ago, but on the screen, your skin looks green, your eyes look hollow, and your shirt looks completely distorted. 

You haven't aged a decade in five minutes. You are experiencing **Webcam Compression and Flat Lighting**. 

Webcams are not professional cameras. They use tiny, low-quality sensors aggressively optimized to compress data. This aggressive compression destroys subtle color nuances, turning highly specific fashion choices into digital disasters. Here is the strict science of dressing for the webcam.

## 1. The Moire Effect (The Stripe Glitch)

The single worst thing you can wear on a video call is a shirt with tiny stripes, houndstooth, or micro-checks.
**The Physics:** A cheap webcam sensor possesses very low resolution. When it tries to read the hundreds of tiny stripes on your shirt, the pixel grid literally cannot keep up. The sensor glitches, creating a nauseating, vibrating rainbow pattern across your chest known as the Moire Effect.
**The Fix:** Never wear tight patterns. Only wear solid blocks of color. 

## 2. Stark White Destroys Exposure

Webcams have incredibly primitive "Auto-Exposure" engines. They frantically adjust the brightness of the screen based on whatever is directly in the center. 
**The Physics:** If you wear a crisp, pristine white shirt, the massive block of bright white enters the webcam sensor. The webcam assumes you are standing next to a nuclear explosion and aggressively darkens the entire screen to compensate. 
**The Outcome:** The white shirt remains visible, but your face is plunged into total darkness, making you look like a shadowy villain.
**The Fix:** Swap pure white for soft cream, ivory, or pale blue. These colors are light enough to look crisp, but dark enough to prevent the webcam from destroying the lighting on your face.

## 3. High Saturation Colors "Bleed"

If you wear a screaming neon pink or incredibly bright safety-orange shirt on a webcam with bad lighting, the compression algorithm will fail to contain the vibrant color. 
**The Physics:** The neon color will digitally "bleed" out of the shirt's boundaries, casting a highly artificial pink or orange glow directly up onto your neck and chin, making you look sick.
**The Fix:** Stick to deeply saturated but non-neon Jewel Tones. Emerald Green, Sapphire Blue, and Deep Plum absorb light and remain mathematically stable on cheap sensors. 

## 4. The Undertone Washout

Webcam lenses natively cast a slightly cool, bluish-gray hue over the image. If your skin is Warm (Autumn/Spring), this cool filter violently clashes with your golden biology, making you look gray and exhausted on screen. 

You must combat this blue filter by aggressively wearing your Warm power colors. Wear Mustard, Terracotta, or warm Olive on camera to manually inject the warmth the camera is stealing from you.
