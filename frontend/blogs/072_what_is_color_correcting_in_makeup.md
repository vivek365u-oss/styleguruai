# What is Color Correcting in Makeup?

For decades, women believed that the secret to flawless makeup was simply buying a thicker, heavier foundation. They would layer thick "full-coverage" makeup over their acne or dark circles, hoping to erase them. The result was almost always a heavy, cakey, uncomfortable mask that cracked within three hours. 

The most elite makeup artists do not use heavy foundation to hide flaws. They use optical physics. 

This technique is called **Color Correcting**. It allows you to use a microscopically thin layer of makeup to completely erase imperfections. Here is the science behind it and exactly how to use it. 

## The Core Concept: Neutralization

Color Correcting relies entirely on the basic Color Wheel. 
The absolute rule of the color wheel is **Simultaneous Neutralization**: Colors that sit on exact opposite ends of the color wheel mathematically cancel each other out. 

If you lay the complementary color over your flaw, both colors turn into a flat, invisible neutral. You do not need to "bury" the imperfection; you simply neutralize its wavelength. 

## The Color Corrector Arsenal

Depending on what issue you are targeting, you must select the precise opposing weapon.

### 1. The Green Corrector (Targets Redness)
Because Green is the exact opposite of Red on the color wheel, a pale green corrector is the ultimate weapon against acne scars, active pimples, Rosacea, and sunburns. 
*How to use:* Apply a tiny dot of green concealer directly onto the red blemish and tap it in until it disappears. The blemish won't look green; the redness will simply vanish into generic skin tone. Then, apply a thin layer of normal foundation over it. 

### 2. The Peach / Orange Corrector (Targets Dark Circles)
Dark under-eye circles, hyperpigmentation, and the dark shadows around the mouth on Indian skin tones are almost entirely composed of Blue and Purple wavelengths beneath the thin skin. 
Because Orange is the exact opposite of Blue, an orange or deep peach corrector completely erases the shadow. (More on this specific Indian skin saving-hack in the next guide).

### 3. The Lavender / Purple Corrector (Targets Sallowness)
Purple is the opposite of Yellow on the color wheel. If your skin is looking incredibly exhausted, sallow, overly-jaundiced, or unnaturally yellow, applying a sheer lavender primer corrects the imbalance. It injects "cool" optical energy into the face, instantly brightening and waking up the skin without adding heavy coverage. 

## The Law of Thin Layers

The biggest mistake amateurs make with Color Correctors is applying them thickly—like a normal concealer. 

Color correctors are highly pigmented chemical agents. You must use exactly *one dot*. If you apply a thick layer of Orange corrector under your eyes, your normal foundation will not be able to cover it, and you will walk around all day with orange circles. The corrector must be a thin, invisible layer embedded into the skin *before* foundation.
